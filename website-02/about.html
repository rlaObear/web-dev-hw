Childhood 


            I had the privilege of growing up barefoot, 
            in a small, unassuming podunk town that sat at the foothills 
            of the majestic Bitterroot Mountains.
            
            Surrounded by nature's beauty, 
            I felt an unbridled sense of freedom and liberation, 
            far removed from the chaos and complexities of the bustling outside world.
            In that tranquil haven, life unfolded at its own unhurried pace. 
            I wandered through meadows, ventured into dense forests, and explored winding trails 
            that led me to hidden treasures. 

            With each step I took, the earth's textures and sensations became intimately familiar, 
            as if forming a profound connection between my very being and the natural world around me.
            The absence of inhibitions allowed my imagination to flourish, 
            untouched by the pressures and distractions of the larger world. 
            I reveled in the simplicity and purity of those moments, 
            where time seemed to stand still, 
            and the worries of the outside world did not exsist.

            Barefoot and carefree, I developed a deep appreciation 
            for the small wonders that adorned my everyday life. 
            Whether it was the whisper of wind through the trees, 
            the gentle babbling of a nearby stream, 
            or the sight of wildflowers painting the landscape, 
            each experience was a testament to the remarkable beauty that can be found 
            when we detach ourselves from the chaos and immerse ourselves in the serenity of nature.
            
            Those years spent in the embrace of the Bitterroot Mountains shaped my perspective on life, 
            nstilling in me an enduring reverence for simplicity, connection, 
            and the inherent harmony of the natural world. Even as I've journeyed beyond that tranquil town, 
            the memories of my barefoot escapades serve as a constant reminder to seek moments of uninhibited bliss, 
            grounding myself in the beauty and tranquility that can still be found if we take the time to look and truly appreciate.
